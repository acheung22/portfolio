"you're a good drawer"

As a child I had these four necessities going out anywhere: a crossbody bag, a bundle of multicolored–sometimes scented–pens, my iPod (and later my phone), and of course, a notebook. I had notebooks of all shapes and sizes, most prominently Japanese character notebooks with the likes of Rilakkuma, Sumikko Gurashi, Sanrio, etc. Sitting in restaurants with my family, football games with my friends, camping, travelling, simply in the car, anywhere. I don't carry a notebook with me at all times anymore, but I figured this can serve to symbolically replace my childhood necessity.

I used to love sitting on the deck of my old house. Especially during the beginning of the pandemic, it was my escape from the chaos. One of my favorite parts about sitting out there was I could hear someone playing tennis every morning, and there was something so soothing about the rhythmic sound of someone hitting a tennis ball back and forth.
